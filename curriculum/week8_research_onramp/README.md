# Week 8 — Paper Reading & the Research On-ramp

> *Goal: the advisor's **Stage 3** — start reading top-venue papers (CVPR / ICCV / ECCV), find a
> direction, and run honest experiments. This is the bridge from "I can run models" to "I'm doing
> research."*

You can train nets (W5), run tooling (W6), and drive real CV/VLM models (W7). The last step is the
research *practice*: reading the literature critically, choosing a question, evaluating models honestly,
and comparing experiments so your conclusions hold up.

| Day | Topic | Format | You'll be able to | Status |
|-----|-------|--------|-------------------|--------|
| 48 | **How to read a paper (CVPR/ICCV/ECCV)** | guided | Read a paper in 3 passes; extract the contribution & the catch | ✅ |
| 49 | **Finding a topic & the literature map** | guided | Turn a vague interest into a question; map the related work | ✅ |
| 50 | **Evaluating models: metrics done right** | pytest | Compute precision/recall/F1 from predictions; avoid the accuracy trap | ✅ |
| 51 | **Experiments, tracking & ablations** | guided | Design a fair comparison; change one thing at a time; log it | ✅ |
| 52 | **Capstone: your mini research project** | guided | Scope a small reproduce-then-tweak project end-to-end | ✅ |

**Format:** mostly **guided** (research is judgment, not unit-testable) — except Day 50, where the
evaluation math *is* gradable and matters enough to build by hand. This week has no single "right
answer"; it hands you the process your advisor and lab expect.

**Where this lands:** by the end you've gone from zero background (Day 1) to being able to read a modern
vision-language paper, reproduce a baseline, evaluate it honestly, and propose a small extension — the
exact footing a master's research project starts from.

**Next:** your own research. This is the on-ramp; the road is yours.
